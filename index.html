John Carmack Tribute Page
John Carmack, Computer Developer

(b. August 21, 1970)

History:

1970: Born in Kansas by Inga and Stan Carmack

1978: Introduced to his first game, Space Invaders

1980: Pac-Man inspires him to become a game
developer, along with Shigeru Miyamoto of Nintendo

1984: Arrested for trying to steal Apple
IIs from a school with other kids

1988: Attends the University of Missouri-Kansas City
for a few semesters before dropping out to become
a freelance programmer

Career:

He was hired by the company Softdisk to work on SoftDisk G-S.
This is where he met Tom Hall, John Romero, and Adrian Carmack.
They were in charge of a subscription item called Gamer's Edge
, while also creating the Commander Keen series, which was published
by Apogee Software. Later, the group left Softdisk and co-founded 'id Software'.
